Key Rituals of a Traditional Wedding

Haldi Ceremony

Haldi is a joyful and sacred pre-wedding ritual where a turmeric paste is applied to the bride and groom by their loved ones. This vibrant ceremony symbolizes purification, protection from evil, and the beginning of a new journey. The yellow color signifies prosperity and is believed to bless the couple with glowing skin and good fortune.

Kanyadaanam Ceremony

Kanyadaanam, meaning “giving away the daughter,” is one of the most sacred rituals in a Hindu wedding. The bride’s parents entrust their daughter to the groom, symbolizing the transfer of responsibility with heartfelt blessings. This emotional ritual highlights the parents’ love, trust, and faith in the groom to care for their daughter with respect and devotion for life.

Sindoor & Mangalsutra

The Sindoor and Mangalsutra rituals are symbols of a married woman’s dignity and devotion. During this sacred moment, the groom applies sindoor (vermilion powder) on the bride’s forehead and ties the mangalsutra (a sacred necklace) around her neck. These acts represent lifelong commitment, love, and spiritual unity, marking the official beginning of married life
